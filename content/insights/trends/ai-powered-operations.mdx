## Implementation Strategies That Work

### Start Small, Scale Fast

<Step number={1} title="Identify High-Impact, Low-Complexity Use Cases">
  Look for repetitive processes with clear rules and available data. Invoice processing and basic
  customer service are excellent starting points.
</Step>

<Step number={2} title="Build Proof of Concept">
  Allocate 2-3 months for a focused POC. Measure everything: time saved, error reduction, cost
  impact.
</Step>

<Step number={3} title="Scale Horizontally">
  Once proven, rapidly deploy similar use cases across departments before tackling more complex
  challenges.
</Step>

### Common Implementation Pitfalls

<DoDont
  dos={[
    "Start with processes that have clear success metrics",
    "Involve end-users from day one",
    "Plan for ongoing model training and improvement",
    "Build explainable AI for trust and adoption",
  ]}
  donts={[
    "Automate broken processes (fix first, then automate)",
    "Ignore change management requirements",
    "Underestimate data quality needs",
    "Promise unrealistic timelines or savings",
  ]}
/>

## The Human Element

<Aside type="warning">
  The most successful AI implementations augment human capabilities rather than replacing humans
  entirely. Focus on human-AI collaboration models.
</Aside>

### Workforce Transformation Strategies

1. **Reskilling Programs**: Invest in training current employees for AI-augmented roles
2. **New Role Creation**: AI trainers, model auditors, automation architects
3. **Cultural Shift**: From "AI will replace us" to "AI will empower us"
