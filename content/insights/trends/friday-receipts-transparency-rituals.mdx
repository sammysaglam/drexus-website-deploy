## What Are Friday Receipts?

Friday Receipts are concise, written updates sent every Friday at 5pm (local time) that answer three
questions:

1. What we shipped this week
2. What's in progress
3. What's blocked or at risk

Unlike status meetings, they're asynchronous, searchable, and create a permanent record of progress.

## The Anatomy of an Effective Friday Receipt

After analyzing 1,200+ Friday Receipts, we've identified the components that drive results:

### Shipped This Week (40% of content)

- Specific features/fixes with evidence (screenshots, metrics)
- Links to deployments or PRs
- User impact clearly stated

### In Progress (35% of content)

- Current sprint work with % complete
- Expected completion dates
- Any discovered complexity

### Blockers & Risks (25% of content)

- Specific blockers with owners
- Proposed solutions or needs
- Escalation triggers defined

## Implementation Patterns Across Company Stages

Different organizations adapt Friday Receipts to their context:

### Seed Stage (5-15 people)

- Single receipt from founder/CTO
- Covers entire company progress
- Sent to team + investors
- Focus on runway impact

### Series A/B (15-50 people)

- Team-level receipts (engineering, product, sales)
- Rolled up to company digest
- Sent to all-hands distribution list
- Focus on OKR progress

### Growth Stage (50-200 people)

- Department receipts with exec summary
- Automated aggregation tools
- Searchable archive required
- Focus on dependencies and blockers

### Enterprise (200+ people)

- Automated collection via tools
- AI-powered summarization
- Integration with project management
- Focus on cross-team coordination

## Getting Started This Friday

Ready to implement Friday Receipts? Here's your week-one playbook:

<Step number={1} title="Choose Your Format">
  Email, Slack post, or shared doc. Pick one and stick with it. Consistency matters more than
  perfection.
</Step>

<Step number={2} title="Set the Time">
  Friday 5pm local time. Non-negotiable. The constraint forces clarity and prevents
  over-engineering.
</Step>

<Step number={3} title="Write Your First Receipt">
  15 minutes max. Cover shipped/progress/blocked. Add one screenshot for evidence. Send to your
  immediate team.
</Step>

<Step number={4} title="Iterate Weekly">
  Ask for feedback. Refine format. Add recipients gradually. Most teams find their groove by week 4.
</Step>
